# KarmaKaart Market Research: Dutch Organizations for Acquisition & Licensing

## Executive Summary

**KarmaKaart has immediate high-value opportunities across seven sectors in the Netherlands, with 50+ qualified prospects and potential market value exceeding €10M annually.** The strongest near-term opportunities are: (1) Provincie Groningen's €100M civic initiative program needing urgent volunteer coordination, (2) ABN AMRO Foundation managing 6,900 volunteers with proven platform budget, (3) Humanitas coordinating 10,000 volunteers across 92 chapters, and (4) housing corporations like Ymere and Haag Wonen with substantial leefbaarheid budgets. The market timing is exceptional due to new January 2025 legislation mandating municipal participation tools, CSRD compliance requirements for corporations, and €3M in available government innovation funding.

**Three viable business models emerge:** Platform acquisition by deep-pocketed organizations (provinces, large corporations), institutional licenses for universities and municipalities, and per-organization licenses for NGOs and housing corporations. Conservative first-year revenue potential: €500K-€1M from 5-10 anchor customers, scaling to €3-5M by year three with 30-50 organizations.

The research identified specific decision-makers, budget lines, and pain points across 100+ organizations. Most critically, few competitors offer KarmaKaart's unique combination of interactive maps, gamification, and check-in functionality—existing solutions like OpenStad lack volunteer-specific features while platforms like NLvoorelkaar lack government integration.
